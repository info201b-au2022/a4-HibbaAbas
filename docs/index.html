Abstract

This report uses r and Ventura data to investigate the U.S jail systems.The Ventura data used has recorded data from 1970 to 2018. In this report the prisons care for the well being of its inmates are examined through the recorded death counts of inmates, the racial disparities in the jail populations, and more are included.

Introduction

America established its prison systems in 1891, but it was in 1970 when it started to incarcerate mass amounts of people. In fact, America has the highest rate of incarceration of its citizen in the world! This is concerning considering that America enjoys political stability and does not have crime rates comparable to ones in other countries around the globe. A growing number of people in the U.S have pointed out the problems and biases with the U.S prison systems, and have pushed for more sustainable reform and alternative options to prison. In this report the issues and biases of the U.S prison systems are explored and then visualized to provide a more lasting and clear impression for the general public.

Data Summary

Americas prison systems are not good, the health of the incarcerated is neglected. From 2000 to 2013 there were 8,981,402 deaths across all prisons in the nation. The amount of deaths would increase with each year, in 2000 there were approximatly 522,556 and in 2013 there were 667,169, meaning there was an 144,613 increase in deaths over thirteen years.
